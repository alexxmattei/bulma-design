Role of Coffee in Programming

From caffeine buzz to debugging lows

Hello World

As you may know, as a programmer, you just need something to keep you from dashing your knuckles into your keyboard when the code just isn't working. For me the only pain reliever is some nice freshly made coffee. Not only that caffeine helps you become more creative and efficient when coding, but when consumed properly it can be healthy too.

Coffee and Networking

For developers, coffee isn't just a way to kick off the day and start working. It's like a simbol for cooperation and willingness to share ideas with others. Coffee shops are one of the best places to write quality code, and it's even better when the usuals are programming. At least it used to be this way before the pandemic but who can you blame. It's pretty easy to say that developers have came a long way only to rely on a handful of tools, especially during the pandemic.

  • Discord
  • YouTube
  • GitHub
  • Stack Overflow
  • And of course coffee!

Coffee addiction

But there is more to the reason that developers just love coffee. And that is because, working at a desk all day long and struggling to meet deadlines can be as physically challenging as it is psychically. This being said I think that sometimes it's easier to push yourself when you have a beaten laptop and three empty coffee mugs on your desk at 3AM.

However, if you want to cut down or give up the need for caffeine here are some things that may help you:

  1. Replace coffee with water or your favourite juice.
  2. Start the day by drinking a lot of fluids.
  3. Replace caffeine based products with decaf or other alternatives.
  4. Also, learn to better search on stackoverflow so you don't need coffee in the first place!
